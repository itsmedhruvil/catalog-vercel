# Quick Analytics Reference Card

## 🎯 How to Access Analytics Features

### **1. Analytics Dashboard**
**Where to click:** Look for the **Analytics** button in your main navigation
- **What you'll see:** Real-time KPIs, charts for sales trends, top products, category distribution
- **Key features:** Filter by date/category/supplier, CSV export

### **2. Inventory Alerts**
**Where to click:** Look for the **Alerts** button (🔔 icon) in your main navigation
- **What you'll see:** 6 types of alerts with severity colors (red/yellow/green)
- **Key features:** Click alerts for details, dismiss resolved ones, configure thresholds

### **3. Delivery Management**
**Where to click:** Look for the **Delivery** button (🚚 icon) in your main navigation
- **What you'll see:** Delivery status tracking, supplier performance, warehouse info
- **Key features:** Update delivery statuses, track lead times, manage suppliers

### **4. Enhanced Product Analytics**
**Where to click:** 
1. Click the **Lock icon** to enable Admin Mode
2. Click **Add Product** or **Edit** on any product
3. Navigate through the tabs at the top

**Tabs you'll see:**
- **Basic Info** - Product details
- **Delivery** - Supplier & lead time info  
- **Warehouse** - Storage & batch tracking
- **Analytics** - Performance metrics & targets
- **Inventory** - Stock levels & reorder points

### **5. Supplier Management**
**Where to click:** Admin Mode → Edit any product → **Delivery** tab
- **What you'll see:** Supplier contact info, lead times, performance metrics
- **Key features:** Track supplier reliability, update contact information

### **6. Warehouse Management**
**Where to click:** Admin Mode → Edit any product → **Warehouse** tab
- **What you'll see:** Bin locations, batch numbers, storage info
- **Key features:** Track warehouse space usage, organize inventory

## 📊 What Each Analytics Feature Shows

### **Sales Analytics**
- 30-day sales trends with growth percentages
- Conversion rates and return rates
- Customer rating trends
- Top 10 performing products

### **Inventory Analytics**
- Stock turnover rates (how fast items sell)
- Reorder point recommendations
- Dead stock identification (items not selling)
- Warehouse space utilization

### **Delivery Analytics**
- Average delivery times by supplier
- Delivery success rates
- Supplier performance comparison
- Lead time trends

## 🚨 Alert Types Explained

1. **Low Stock** (🔴 Red) - Items below reorder point
2. **High Stock** (🟡 Yellow) - Items overstocked
3. **Sales Decline** (🔴 Red) - Products with dropping sales
4. **Delivery Delays** (🔴 Red) - Late deliveries from suppliers
5. **Low Ratings** (🟡 Yellow) - Products with poor customer ratings
6. **Expiry Dates** (🔴 Red) - Items approaching expiration

## 🎛️ Dashboard Controls

**Filter Controls:**
- **Date Range:** Last 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, custom range
- **Category:** Filter by product categories
- **Supplier:** Filter by specific suppliers

**Export Options:**
- **CSV Export:** Download all dashboard data
- **Chart Downloads:** Save individual charts as images

## 📱 Mobile Access

All analytics features work on mobile:
- Dashboard charts resize automatically
- Alert notifications are mobile-friendly
- Delivery tracking works with touch
- Product forms optimized for phone screens

## 🔧 Quick Setup for First-Time Use

1. **Enable Admin Mode** (click lock icon)
2. **Add a test product** with complete info
3. **Set supplier and warehouse details**
4. **Configure alert thresholds** in settings
5. **Generate some sample data** to see analytics work
6. **Click Analytics** to see your dashboard

## 💡 Pro Tips

- **Real-time updates:** Data refreshes every 30 seconds
- **Alert customization:** Adjust thresholds based on your business needs
- **Export data:** Use CSV export for external analysis
- **Mobile alerts:** Get notified on your phone for important alerts
- **Supplier tracking:** Monitor which suppliers are most reliable

## 🆘 Need Help?

If analytics aren't showing data:
1. Make sure you have products with sales information
2. Check that delivery dates are filled in
3. Verify inventory levels are set
4. Confirm supplier information is complete

If alerts aren't triggering:
1. Check your alert threshold settings
2. Ensure inventory data is accurate
3. Verify delivery dates are set correctly
